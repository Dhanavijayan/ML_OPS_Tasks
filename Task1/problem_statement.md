# Supermarket Theft Identification

## Problem Statement

Supermarket theft causes significant losses to the retail industry each year. Early identification and prevention of theft can have a substantial impact on reducing these losses. The goal of this task is to develop a solution for identifying thefts in supermarket environments through data-driven or computational approaches. This may involve analyzing transaction logs, surveillance data, customer purchase patterns, or other available signals to detect potential theft events.

### Objectives
- Clearly define the types of theft scenarios to be addressed (e.g., shoplifting, employee theft, ticket switching).
- Explore and outline possible data sources that can be leveraged for theft detection.
- Propose algorithms or methodologies for identifying suspicious activities.
- Suggest metrics for evaluating the efficacy of the identification system.

### Expected Deliverables
- A clear written problem statement (this document).
- Summary of data requirements and possible acquisition strategies.
- Proposed approach(es) for detection and identification.
- Discussion of potential challenges and mitigation strategies.
